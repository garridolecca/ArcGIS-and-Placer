How to Use This App

Getting Started

This platform visualizes Placer AI foot traffic data for 1,156 retail locations across 24 major brands in the Los Angeles metro area. It combines real foot traffic analytics with ArcGIS GeoEnrichment demographics to help evaluate retail site performance.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1
Choose your mode: On the launch screen, enter an ArcGIS API Key for live demographic data from the GeoEnrichment service, or click "Explore with Demo Data" to use simulated demographics alongside real Placer AI traffic data.
2
Select a retail brand: Use the "Retail Brand" dropdown to choose from 24 brands (Target, Costco, Whole Foods, Nordstrom, etc.). The map will highlight all locations for that brand with diamond markers.
3
Pick a store location: The "Store Location" dropdown lists all stores for the selected brand, sorted by foot traffic. Each entry shows the city, address, and average weekly visits.
4
Set the analysis radius: Choose 0.5, 1, 2, 3, or 5 miles. This defines the trade area buffer around the selected location.
5
Click "Analyze Store": The app draws a buffer on the map and runs two analyses in parallel: ArcGIS GeoEnrichment for area demographics, and a spatial query of all Placer AI locations within the buffer.

Understanding the Results

  • Site Score (0-100): A composite score based on four weighted factors: Foot Traffic (30%), Affluence (25%), Market Size (25%), and Dwell Time (20%). Scores above 70 indicate strong sites; below 45 suggest caution.
  • Area Demographics: Population, households, median age, income, and spending data from ArcGIS GeoEnrichment (or simulated in demo mode). Toggle enrichment variable categories in the "Enrichment Variables" section.
  • Foot Traffic Intelligence: Total weekly visits and unique visitors within the buffer, average dwell time, visitor median household income (from Placer AI), day-of-week patterns, top brands by traffic, and category mix breakdown.
  • Strategic Insights: Automated recommendations based on traffic density, income levels, dwell time, retail density, population size, and visitor demographics.

Custom Location Analysis

Enable the "Click map to analyze custom location" toggle to analyze any point on the map - not just existing store locations. This is useful for evaluating potential new sites or competitor locations.

Visualization Options

  • Heatmap View: Toggle in "Layer Controls" to switch from individual points to a density heatmap showing foot traffic concentration.
  • Brand Highlights: Toggle to show/hide the diamond markers for the selected brand's locations.
  • Point Symbology: Point size and color represent foot traffic volume - larger/redder points indicate higher traffic; smaller/bluer points indicate lower traffic.

Enrichment Variables

Check or uncheck categories to control which demographic variables are fetched:

  • Demographics: Population, households, average household size, median age
  • Income & Wealth: Median/average household income, per capita income
  • Consumer Spending: Apparel and grocery spending indices
  • Daytime Population: Total daytime pop, workers, and residents (useful for identifying employment centers)
  • Housing: Owners, renters, median/average home values
  • Custom Variable: Enter any valid ArcGIS GeoEnrichment variable ID (e.g., KeyUSFacts.DIVINDX_CY for Diversity Index)

Publishing to ArcGIS Online

Use the "Publish to ArcGIS Online" section to upload the full Placer AI dataset as a hosted feature service. Enter your ArcGIS Online credentials and portal URL, then click "Publish Data to AGOL". The data will be shared publicly and can be used in other ArcGIS applications, dashboards, and Story Maps.

Data Sources

  • Foot Traffic: Placer AI (March 2025) - weekly visits, dwell time, unique visitors, visit frequency, visitor HHI
  • Demographics: ArcGIS GeoEnrichment Service (Esri) or simulated demo data
  • Basemap: ArcGIS Dark Gray Vector (API key mode) or CartoDB Dark Matter (demo mode)
Pro Tip: Compare multiple locations by running analyses sequentially - the side panel updates with each new analysis while previous buffer outlines remain visible on the map until the next analysis clears them.
